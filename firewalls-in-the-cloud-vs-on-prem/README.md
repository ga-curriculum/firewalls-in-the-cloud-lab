## Comparative analysis of cloud and on-premise firewalls

Your next challenge is to build on your existing research by analyzing the fundamental differences between firewalls in the cloud and traditional on-premise firewall appliances.

To support your analysis, consider the following key factors:

- **Architecture:** Examine how on-premise firewalls are typically hardware or virtual appliances deployed within physical networks versus the cloud-native firewalls integrated into virtual network infrastructures (for example, AWS VPC or Azure VNet).

  Consider how this affects traffic flow, integration points, and security policy application.

- **Deployment models:** On-premise deployments often involve physical installation and manual configuration, while cloud firewalls can be provisioned rapidly using cloud consoles or infrastructure-as-code tools.

- **Scalability:** Evaluate how each model handles changing network demands. On-premise solutions may require purchasing additional hardware, while cloud firewalls often offer on-demand scalability to support fluctuating workloads or global distribution.

- **Management and monitoring:** Explore how cloud-based firewalls offer centralized dashboards, automation capabilities, and integration with cloud-native monitoring tools, in contrast to traditional models that may rely on local management consoles or require VPN access for remote management.

- **Operational overhead:** Identify the administrative and maintenance differences. On-premise firewalls require ongoing hardware maintenance, updates, and physical security, while cloud firewalls reduce this burden by shifting infrastructure responsibilities to the cloud provider.

Create a comparative analysis document that outlines the key distinctions you identify across these dimensions. A side-by-side table may be a helpful format.

Finally, reflect on the implications of these differences for organizations transitioning from on-premise to cloud-based network security. Consider how this shift may impact staffing, security models, cost structures, compliance, and the organization’s overall approach to risk management.
